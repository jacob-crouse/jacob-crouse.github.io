Experience

Graduate Research Assistant

Penn State Unmanned Research Laboratory

Since August, I have enacted both software and hardware changes to multiple unmanned vehicles under the guidance of my advisor, Dr. Eric Johnson.

I have outfitted a commercially-available hobby-level fixed wing with electronics necessary for unmanned flight. This included receiver and flight controller setup using open-source flight control software, as well as a wireless communications link for use with a ground station for remote monitoring and commanding autonomous waypoints.

Additionally, I have developed our own fork of the open-source Betaflight flight control software for multirotor autonomous flight. The fork includes additions of serial read and write commands for sending sensor and pilot input data, and auto/manual logic for switching between manual and automatic flight.

August 2018 - Present

Engineering Intern

National Institute of Standards and Technology

As an undergraduate, I looked to place myself as quickly as possible into a position where I could experience a professional engineering environment.

As a fresh intern, I spent the first summer becoming familiar with first responders and their missions, problems, and ideas for new technology. This research culminated in my first publication: The Next-Generation Firefighter: The Evolution of Technology.

During the rest of my tenure at NIST, I created technology that would demonstrate potential improvements to the current technology used by first responders. To accomplish this, I created a Lego model of the physical layer of LTE (cellular) downlink to show first responders just how much more advanced LTE was when compared with traditional Land Mobile Radios (LMR). Additionally, I implemented an HTML dashboard which was utilized to display the location and vital statistics of a first responder equipped with a sensor harness to monitor their health.
